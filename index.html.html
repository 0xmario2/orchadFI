Getting Started

Start Earning in 3 Steps

OrchardFi lets any wallet earn optimized DeFi yield with no active management required. Follow these steps to deposit your first position.

1
Connect Your Wallet Click "Launch App" and connect via MetaMask, WalletConnect, or Coinbase Wallet. OrchardFi is non-custodial — your keys, your funds.
2
Choose a Pool Browse available yield pools filtered by risk level, chain, and APY. Each pool shows live TVL, volume, and estimated returns.
3
Deposit & Harvest Enter your deposit amount, approve the token, and confirm the transaction. The Yield Engine auto-compounds your rewards every 8–12 hours.

💡 No lock-ups: All OrchardFi positions are withdrawable at any time with no early exit penalties.

Supported Wallets

  • MetaMask — Browser extension & mobile app
  • WalletConnect — Any WC-compatible mobile wallet
  • Coinbase Wallet — Desktop & mobile
  • Rabby Wallet — Multi-chain browser extension
  • Safe (Gnosis) — Multisig support for teams & DAOs

Minimum Deposit

There is no minimum deposit enforced at the protocol level. However, gas costs on Ethereum mainnet mean positions under $50 may not be economically efficient. Consider Arbitrum or BNB Chain pools for smaller positions.

How It Works

Protocol Architecture

OrchardFi operates as a yield aggregation layer sitting on top of established DeFi primitives (Uniswap v3, Curve, GMX). Deposited assets flow through the Vault → Router → Strategy stack.

FLOW 
User Wallet
    │
    ▼
OrchardVault (ERC-4626)
    │   Accepts deposits, mints oTokens
    ▼
YieldRouter
    │   Selects optimal strategy allocation
    ▼
Strategy Contracts
    ├── UniswapV3Strategy
    ├── CurveStrategy
    └── GMXStrategy
    │
    ▼
Auto-Compound Engine
    │   Harvests → Swaps → Re-deposits
    ▼
User receives compounded yield

oTokens (Receipt Tokens)

When you deposit into a pool, you receive oTokens (e.g., oETH-USDC) representing your share of the vault. These tokens:

  • Appreciate in value as yield compounds — you don't need to "claim" rewards manually
  • Are fully transferable and composable with other DeFi protocols
  • Can be used as collateral in partner lending markets
  • Are burned upon withdrawal to receive the underlying + earned yield

Fee Structure

  • Performance Fee: 10% of yield harvested (deducted before compounding)
  • Management Fee: 0% — no annual drag on principal
  • Withdrawal Fee: 0% — always free to exit

Performance fees flow to the protocol treasury and are governed by $ORCH token holders.

Core Technology

The Orchard Yield Engine

The Yield Engine is OrchardFi's automated compounding system. It monitors all active positions and harvests rewards when the compound frequency maximizes net APY after gas costs.

Compound Frequency Algorithm

The engine calculates optimal harvest timing using:

MATH 
Optimal Harvest When:
  PendingRewards × RewardPrice > GasCost × GasPrice × HarvestThreshold

Where HarvestThreshold = 1.15 (15% efficiency buffer)

Effective APY = (1 + r/n)^n - 1
  r = base APY
  n = compound frequency per year (avg ~1,095 / 3x daily)

Strategy Selection

Deposited capital is deployed across multiple sub-strategies simultaneously, weighted by expected risk-adjusted return. The allocation rebalances every 24 hours based on:

  • Current APY signals from each underlying protocol
  • Liquidity depth to prevent slippage on large deposits
  • Smart contract risk score from internal audit classification
  • Correlation with existing pool exposure to manage IL

⚠️ Impermanent Loss: Volatile pairs (e.g., ORCH/ETH) are exposed to IL. The engine does not hedge IL by default — consider this risk before depositing into high-APY pools.

Keeper Network

Harvest transactions are executed by a decentralized keeper network. Any address may call harvest() on a strategy contract and receive a 0.1% keeper reward from the harvested amount. This ensures censorship-resistant, permissionless operation even if the OrchardFi team is unavailable.

Upgradeability

Vault contracts use a transparent proxy pattern with a 48-hour timelock on all upgrades. Strategy contracts are non-upgradeable — new strategies are deployed separately and whitelisted via governance vote. This limits blast radius in the event of a bug.

Security

Security is the highest priority for OrchardFi. We apply defense-in-depth across code, infrastructure, and protocol design.

Security Measures

  • Multisig Admin: All privileged admin functions require 3-of-5 multisig approval from the core team Safe wallet
  • Timelock: 48-hour delay on all contract upgrades and parameter changes
  • Reentrancy Guards: All external calls are protected with OpenZeppelin ReentrancyGuard
  • Price Oracle: Chainlink TWAP used exclusively — no spot pricing susceptible to flash loan manipulation
  • Emergency Pause: Guardian role can pause deposits/withdrawals within a single tx if an exploit is detected

🛡️ Bug Bounty: OrchardFi runs an active bug bounty on Immunefi with up to $250,000 in rewards for critical vulnerabilities. Submit a report →

Risks to Understand

DeFi is inherently risky. Even audited protocols can have undiscovered bugs. Before depositing, understand:

  • Smart Contract Risk: Bugs may exist despite audits
  • Impermanent Loss: Volatile pair pools can lose value vs holding
  • Protocol Dependency Risk: OrchardFi relies on Uniswap, Curve, and other upstream protocols
  • Oracle Risk: Chainlink downtime or manipulation could affect strategy rebalancing
  • Regulatory Risk: DeFi regulation is evolving — consult your local laws
FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Is OrchardFi non-custodial? ▼
Yes. OrchardFi never holds custody of your funds in a centralized manner. All assets are held in on-chain smart contracts that you interact with directly. The OrchardFi team cannot access, freeze, or move your deposited funds — only the Vault contracts can, according to their programmed logic.
How often are rewards compounded? ▼
The Yield Engine compounds rewards every 8–12 hours for most pools, depending on gas conditions and pending reward size. During periods of high gas prices, the compounding frequency may drop to once daily. You can view the last harvest timestamp for each pool in the app. You never need to manually claim or reinvest — it happens automatically.
Can I withdraw at any time? ▼
Yes. There are no lock-up periods, timelocks, or withdrawal penalties on any OrchardFi pool. You can withdraw your full position (principal + accrued yield) at any time. The only cost is the network gas fee for the withdrawal transaction. In emergencies, the protocol may be paused by the Guardian multisig, which could temporarily delay withdrawals — but this has never occurred.
What is the difference between APY and APR? ▼
APR (Annual Percentage Rate) is the simple interest rate without compounding. APY (Annual Percentage Yield) accounts for compounding frequency. OrchardFi displays APY, which reflects the actual effective return assuming rewards are automatically reinvested — which they are, by the Yield Engine. An APR of 20% compounded daily results in approximately 22.1% APY.
Does OrchardFi charge any fees? ▼
OrchardFi charges a 10% performance fee on harvested yield only. This fee is taken before compounding and goes to the protocol treasury (governed by $ORCH holders). There are no management fees, deposit fees, or withdrawal fees. The APY displayed in the app is already net of the performance fee — what you see is what you get.
What chains does OrchardFi support? ▼
OrchardFi currently supports three chains: Ethereum Mainnet, BNB Chain, and Arbitrum One. Each chain hosts its own set of vault and strategy contracts. Cross-chain deposits are not currently supported — you must switch your wallet network to deposit into pools on a specific chain. Expansion to Base and Optimism is planned for Q2 2025.
